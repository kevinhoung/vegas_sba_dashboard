What this dashboard is

A view of every SBA 7(a) loan approved in Clark County, Nevada from FY2020 through March 2026 (the most recent SBA FOIA release), filtered to your acquisition-focused criteria. Restaurants and food service (NAICS 722) are excluded throughout per your request.

How "recession resilience" is measured

For each industry, we look at SBA 7(a) loans approved in FY2020 through FY2023 (loans that have had at least roughly 2 years to season) and compute the share that ended up charged off. Cancelled loans are excluded from the denominator. The FY2020–2023 window includes the COVID-19 shock and the 2022–2024 interest rate spike, so it's a useful real-world stress test for which industries survive turbulence.

Caveats & what this doesn't tell you

  • SBA 7(a) loans are a sample of small business activity, not the whole picture. Cash businesses and self-funded deals don't show up.
  • "Change of Ownership" is the SBA's tag for acquisition financing, but it doesn't reveal seller-financing structure. You'll need to confirm seller carry directly with brokers and lenders.
  • Charge-off rate is about whether the SBA loan failed, not whether the business failed. The two are correlated but not identical (some businesses sell or refinance out).
  • Loan amount ≠ business revenue. The 75th percentile loan is ~$535K, but acquired businesses often have revenue 1.5–4× the loan amount.
  • The data only goes back to FY2020, so we don't have a 2008-style recession in this dataset. COVID and the rate spike are our best stress tests.

  • The bkstudy and lender activity files were inspected but their pre-aggregated views overlap with what we computed directly from the FOIA data, so they aren't relied on for the main charts.

Suggested next steps

  • Pick 2–3 industries from the "Best-fit" table and search BizBuySell, BizQuest, and LoopNet for live Vegas listings in those NAICS codes.
  • Email or call 2–3 of the top lenders here to pre-qualify before you make offers. Live Oak, Newtek, Byline, and Readycap are usually responsive to acquisition deals.
  • For any specific target, ask the seller for 3 years of tax returns (Schedule C or 1120-S), bank statements, and a current AR aging. The SBA will require these anyway.
  • Build a quick DSCR model: NOI ÷ (proposed debt service) should be ≥ 1.25x to clear SBA underwriting.
